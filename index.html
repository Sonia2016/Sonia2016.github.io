My primary research interests lie at the intersection of Human Behavior, Natural Language Processing, and Machine Learning. In our lab, we design intelligent systems for understanding the dynamics and personalization of health and well-being. I am especially interested in building novel computational methods and models to employ social media, responsibly and ethically, and physiological data toward improving our health and well-being. Among different well-being concerns, I am focusing on mental and psychological disorders, Human immunodeficiency virus (HIV), and Diabetes.

I am a fourth-year PhD student in the Department of System and Information Engineering at the University of Virginia where I am part of the Sensing Systems for Health lab. I received my master and bachelor in Computer Science at the Amirkabir University of Technology (Tehran Polytechnic) in 2013 advised by Prof. S. Mehdi Hashemi. Here is a link to my curriculum vitae.
